Proteome and phosphoproteome analysis of human LRRK2 Drosophila model of Parkinson’s disease

Dec, 2021
Comprehensive proteome analysis of human LRRK2 Drosophila model of Parkinson’s disease

The project involved introducing the LRRK2 (R1441C) variant into Drosophila, observing phenotypic effects, analyzing proteomic and phosphoproteomic changes, quantifying proteins, comparing these changes with other models, and conducting a protein-protein interaction screen to understand Parkinson's Disease mechanisms.

Contribution

In this project, I conducted a detailed analysis of the extensive proteomic and phosphoproteomic datasets generated via Mass Spectrometry, using MaxQuant software. To further enhance our understanding, I applied advanced machine learning techniques to identify critical patterns among nearly 3,000 proteins and phosphorylation sites. These insights were then translated into sophisticated data visualizations that clarified the complex protein dynamics involved. The work provided crucial insights into the molecular drivers of neurodegeneration in PD, identifying potential biomarkers and therapeutic targets. This laid the groundwork for developing more precise interventions, demonstrating the power of combining advanced data analysis with machine learning to advance our understanding and treatment of neurodegenerative diseases.
